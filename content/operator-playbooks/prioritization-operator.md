# Prioritization Operator Playbook

## Purpose

Use this playbook to run a prioritization process that compares bets honestly, exposes opportunity cost, and produces a defensible near-term choice.

## Run This When

- too many initiatives are competing for the same capacity
- stakeholders keep escalating requests into the roadmap
- the team needs a more credible way to make tradeoffs

## Who Should Be In The Room

- PM owner
- engineering lead
- design lead when user impact is material
- finance, operations, or go-to-market partner when commercial implications matter
- decision-maker if the group cannot only recommend

## Timebox

45 to 75 minutes for the review, depending on the number of items.

## Inputs Required

- current candidate initiatives
- current strategy or focus areas
- available capacity assumptions
- supporting evidence for each major bet
- known obligations or protected work

## Expected Output

- categorized candidate list
- clear decision on what is in, out, and protected
- named displacement for every approved bet
- brief rationale that can be reused with stakeholders

## Workflow

### Step 1: Separate protected work first

Before comparing bets, isolate work that must be funded regardless.

Examples:

- compliance
- critical reliability work
- contractual commitments

Rule:

Do not let protected work compete with strategic bets in the same scoring discussion.

### Step 2: Categorize the remaining bets

Use categories such as:

- growth
- retention
- expansion
- platform
- operational efficiency

Rule:

If an item does not fit a category, the item is probably underspecified.

### Step 3: Score expected value

For each item, answer:

- What outcome improves?
- How meaningful is the upside?
- How repeatable is the value if this works?

Use a simple scale such as low, medium, or high.

### Step 4: Score confidence

For each item, answer:

- What evidence do we have?
- What assumptions are carrying the case?
- How likely is it that the upside is overstated?

Rule:

Do not reward conviction. Reward evidence.

### Step 5: Score cost and focus load

For each item, answer:

- What is the delivery cost?
- What coordination cost does this create?
- What focus does it consume beyond engineering time?

### Step 6: Check strategic fit

For each item, answer:

- Does this support the current strategy?
- Does it strengthen a priority metric?
- Does it help the team win where it already chose to compete?

### Step 7: Force displacement

For every item you want to approve, answer:

What specifically are we not doing if we fund this now?

Rule:

No item is approved until its displacement is named.

### Step 8: Finalize the decision set

Create three lists:

- approved now
- deferred
- protected capacity

Add one sentence of rationale for each approved item.

## Failure Modes

- protected work is mixed into bet scoring
- executives bypass the framework through urgency language
- confidence scores are inflated by politics
- the team approves items without naming what gets displaced

## Done Standard

This playbook is complete when the team can explain:

- why the chosen bets won
- what got deferred
- what capacity is already committed
- what tradeoffs were accepted

# Validation Checklist

## Positioning

- A new visitor can identify the audience within the hero and intro.
- A new visitor can explain the promise in one sentence: make better product bets.
- The primary next step is obvious: use a playbook now.

## Tone

- The writing is commercially focused.
- The copy is direct without sounding aggressive.
- The point of view is personal without drifting into autobiography.
- The claims are confident without sounding inflated.

## Usefulness

- Each playbook states the problem it solves.
- Each playbook explains when to use it.
- Each playbook includes a practical framework.
- Each playbook contains a realistic anonymized example.
- Each playbook includes a worksheet or checklist.

## System Cohesion

- Strategy, prioritization, and metrics feel like one operating system.
- Tradeoffs, decision quality, and commercial logic are consistent themes across all pages.
- The framework index makes it easy to choose the right entry point.

## Integration Review

- CTA labels can map cleanly into the live website navigation.
- Section blocks are easy to convert into CMS or MDX components.
- The content works without requiring a newsletter or consulting CTA.

## Visual Review

- The first screen communicates audience, promise, and next action without requiring scrolling.
- The page hierarchy is readable through typography, spacing, and module treatment alone.
- Framework pages support scan-first reading through summary bands, callouts, and structured modules.
- Operator mode is visually distinct from framework mode without feeling like a separate product.
- The design feels premium editorial, not generic SaaS, not self-help, and not dashboard-heavy.
- Mobile layouts preserve the same reading hierarchy and CTA clarity as desktop.
